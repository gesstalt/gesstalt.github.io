This interdisciplinary program is Data Science first, Economics applications second

Reality Check: This is NOT traditional economics with some programming. Read everything below before making your decision.

If you want Economics-focused studies, choose Economics and Business Analytics combination instead. This is in all practical possibilities a Data Science Major with Economics minor

What Data Science and Economics Actually Involves Here

Forget traditional economics. This program combines rigorous mathematical foundations with advanced computational methods and statistical analysis. You'll study alongside CS and Applied Mathematics students with identical expectations. This is a major shift as per the new currivulum.

Data Science Core

Machine learning, statistical modeling, data structures, algorithms, big data analytics

Master the computational and statistical methods to extract insights from complex datasets at scale.

Advanced Mathematics

Linear algebra, calculus, probability theory, optimization, mathematical statistics

Develop the mathematical sophistication required for advanced data science and econometric methods.

Programming & Computation

Python, R, SQL, algorithms, data structures, computational methods

Build sophisticated computational skills to implement data science algorithms and handle large-scale analysis.

Quantitative Economics

Econometrics, time series analysis, behavioral economics, market analysis

Apply data science methods to economic questions, using computational tools to analyze market behavior and policy impact.

Program Focus: Data Science First, Economics Applications Second

This is a single interdisciplinary program with no major/minor options. The curriculum emphasis is:

70%

Data Science Core

Machine learning, statistical modeling, algorithms, programming, mathematical foundations

Primary Focus: You'll spend most of your time mastering hardcore data science methods and computational techniques.

30%

Economics Applications

Econometrics, behavioral economics, market analysis, policy evaluation

Application Domain: Economics serves as the context for applying your data science expertise.

Wrong Program? Consider This Instead

If your primary interest is Economics and you want to learn some CS/Data Science tools as supporting skills, you should choose "Economics and Business Analytics" combination. That program puts Economics first with analytics as tools.

This program puts Data Science first with Economics as the application area.

Prerequisite Alert: Mathematics Foundation

Grade 12 Math = Absolutely Mandatory

Data Science is mathematically intensive. You'll be taking the same rigorous math courses as Applied Mathematics and Computer Science students.

Required: Strong Grade 12 Mathematics Background

You need solid performance in Grade 12 calculus, algebra, and statistics. Data science algorithms require calculus for optimization, linear algebra for machine learning, and statistics for inference.

Co-taught Classes: Same Standards as CS/Applied Math

Many of your courses will be taught alongside Computer Science and Applied Mathematics students. You'll face identical mathematical rigor, programming assignments, and theoretical depth. There are no "easier" versions for DS&E students.

Strategic Course Selection Tips for DS&E

  • Programming Foundation: CSIT101 (Introduction to Python) prepares you for the core CSIT111 course
  • Mathematical Preparation: Strong performance in MATH151 (Introductory Calculus) is essential for data science algorithms
  • Statistical Foundation: MATH103 (Elements of Probability) directly supports statistical modeling and machine learning
  • Computational Thinking: CSIT104 (Introduction to Computer Science) develops algorithmic thinking crucial for data science
  • Logical Reasoning: MATH155 (Mathematical Logic and Proofs) builds the analytical rigor needed for advanced coursework

Remember: This is Data Science, Not Traditional Economics

Your coursework will emphasize computational methods, statistical theory, and mathematical modeling over traditional economic theory. Expect programming assignments, algorithm analysis, and mathematical rigor comparable to Computer Science programs.

Self-Assessment Checklist

Answer yes to every question before declaring Data Science and Economics:

  • Did I excel in Grade 12 mathematics, including calculus and statistics?
  • Am I excited about programming and computational problem-solving?
  • Do I enjoy working with data, finding patterns, and building predictive models?
  • Am I comfortable with the same mathematical rigor as CS and Applied Math students?
  • Am I more interested in data science methods than economic theory? (If economics theory is your main interest, choose Economics and Business Analytics instead)
  • Do I want to learn economics through a quantitative, data-driven lens rather than traditional economic theory?
  • Can I handle intensive programming assignments alongside complex mathematical concepts?

If you answered "no" to any item

Consider Economics and Business Analytics, traditional Economics, or other programs. This Data Science program requires exceptional mathematical and computational readiness—there are no shortcuts or "easier" paths.
