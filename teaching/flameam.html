Is Applied Mathematics Right for You?

Understanding what Applied Math really involves at our university

Reality Check: Applied Mathematics is far more than basic calculations. Read everything below before making your decision.

What Applied Mathematics Actually Involves Here

Basic arithmetic and algebra are just the foundation, not the discipline. Our Applied Mathematics curriculum delves deep into advanced mathematical theory, modeling, computational methods, and statistical analysis.

Mathematical Modeling

Differential equations, dynamical systems, optimization, fluid mechanics

Create mathematical representations of real-world phenomena and predict their behavior using rigorous analytical methods.

Statistics & Probability

Stochastic processes, Bayesian analysis, multivariate statistics, data analysis

Analyze uncertainty, make inferences from data, and build predictive models using advanced statistical theory.

Computational Mathematics

Numerical methods, algorithms, complex analysis, functional analysis

Solve mathematical problems that have no closed-form solutions using computational approaches and advanced algorithms.

Pure Mathematical Theory

Real analysis, abstract algebra, linear algebra, differential geometry

Develop rigorous mathematical reasoning and proof techniques that underpin all applied mathematical work.

Prerequisite Alert: Mathematics Foundation

Strong Grade 12 Math = Absolutely Essential

Applied Mathematics is MORE mathematically intensive than Computer Science. If you struggled with high school math, this program will be overwhelming.

Required: Strong Grade 12 Mathematics Performance

You must have excelled (not just passed) in Grade 12 calculus, trigonometry, and algebra. Our first-year courses assume mastery of these topics and move rapidly into advanced material.

Why Mathematical Maturity is Critical

Applied Mathematics requires abstract thinking, rigorous logical reasoning, and comfort with complex proofs. You'll work with multi-dimensional problems, infinite series, and theoretical concepts that build on years of mathematical development.

First-Year Course Pathways

Before selecting any other courses, understand this critical requirement:

MANDATORY CORE REQUIREMENT

MATH155: Introduction to Mathematical Logic and Proofs

WITHOUT THIS COURSE, YOU CANNOT TAKE ANY SECOND-YEAR APPLIED MATH COURSES

Available in Term 1 and Term 2 - Choose one offering

Term 3 Courses

  • CSIT101: Introduction to Python

Term 4 Courses

  • MATH155: Introduction to Mathematical Logic and Proofs (Core)

Strategic Course Selection Tips for Applied Math

  • Mathematical Foundation: Take MATH151 (Introductory Calculus) in Term 1 or 2 - it's essential for MATH201 (Calculus of One Variable) in Term 3
  • Logic & Proofs: MATH155 is non-negotiable and must be completed before any second-year mathematics courses
  • Computational Skills: CSIT101 (Introduction to Python) prepares you for advanced computational methods
  • Statistical Foundation: MATH103 (Elements of Probability) sets you up for Second year courses.
  • Sequential Learning: Note the mathematical progression - Introductory Calculus → Calculus of One Variable → Calculus of Several Variables

Important Note About Course Sequencing

Applied Mathematics has a highly sequential curriculum. Missing prerequisites will create bottlenecks in later terms. Plan your first-year courses carefully to ensure smooth progression into the advanced mathematics sequence starting in Term 3.

Self-Assessment Checklist

Answer yes to every question before declaring Applied Mathematics MAJOR or MINOR

  • Did I excel (not just pass) in Grade 12 mathematics, particularly calculus?
  • Am I comfortable with abstract mathematical concepts and eager to learn rigorous proof techniques?
  • Do I find satisfaction in solving complex, multi-step problems that may take hours or days?
  • Am I interested in the theoretical foundations behind mathematical applications, not just computational methods?
  • Can I handle courses that are proof-heavy and require deep mathematical reasoning?

If you answered "no" to any item

Consider other quantitative fields or discuss alternatives with an academic advisor. Applied Mathematics demands exceptional mathematical maturity and persistence.

Ready to Make an Informed Decision?

Applied Mathematics is intellectually demanding but extraordinarily rewarding. You'll develop the quantitative reasoning skills to tackle complex problems in science, finance, technology, and research—skills that are increasingly valuable in our data-driven world.
